Introduction¶

A person makes a doctor appointment, receives all the instructions and no-show. Whom to blame?

The data talks about the people who booked medical appointments and wether they showed up or not.

The data consists of 14 characteristics of a patient who booked an appointment. Among them, no-show feature is most import one which says wether they showed up for the appointment or no.

The features are:
$PatientId$ - Identification of a patient
$AppointmentID$ - Identification of each appointment
$Gender$ - Male or Female
$ScheduledDay$ - The day of the actuall appointment, when they have to visit the doctor.
$AppointmentDay$ - The day someone called or registered the appointment, this is before appointment of course.
$Age$ - How old is the patient.
$Neighbourhood$ - Where the appointment takes place.
$Scholarship$ - True of False
$Hipertension$ - True or False
$Diabetes$ - True or False
$Alcoholism$ - True or False
$Handcap$ - True or False
$SMS received$ - 1 or more messages sent to the patient.
$No-show$ - True or False.

Questions i have made clear to answer about the dataset are:

  • Are the Show / No-Show of appointments are related to Gender?
  • Which neighbourhood have higher appointments and show/No-show in them
  • how good messaging appointment people helped in show up for appointment data?

Limitations¶

  • If the reason for medical appointment is given we could be clear and use diseases to get more understanding
  • Might be data is highly colected from a single neighborhood where we cannot differentiate if neighborhood played anyrole in appointments no-show.

Conclusions¶

  • Genderwise appointments are more with female, where female takes care health more than male.
  • Highest appointments are from Jardim Camburi and no follow-up trend wether to get info from neighborhood base for no-show up.
  • SMS_recived should be more elaborated such ha show many messages are sent.
  • Majority of the appointments booked on the weeekdays (Tuesday Wednesday) when compared to weekends (Saturday). Might be middle of weekdays are the best for appointments to book and showup
